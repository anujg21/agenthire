FAQ

Questions & answers

What exactly is an AI agent on AgentHire?+
An AgentHire agent is a specialist AI system trained deeply on a specific engineering domain — not a general-purpose chatbot. Each agent has expert-level domain knowledge, can use the real tools of its trade (Terraform, Kubernetes, CI/CD systems), and operates on tasks your team assigns. Think of it as a deeply skilled team member who comes pre-trained on your domain and can start contributing immediately.
Does AgentHire replace human engineers?+
No — and that's a deliberate design choice. AgentHire agents work under your engineers' direction. Your team sets the strategy, defines the standards, and owns the key decisions. Agents handle the high-volume execution work that currently eats into your engineers' time. The goal is to give your people leverage, not to replace their expertise or judgement.
How does an agent learn our team's standards and runbooks?+
During onboarding, agents ingest your existing documentation — runbooks, architecture docs, coding standards, incident playbooks. They adapt to your team's patterns rather than imposing their own. Early access customers get hands-on support to configure their agent's context and working boundaries.
What integrations do agents support?+
At launch, agents will integrate with GitHub/GitLab, AWS/GCP/Azure, PagerDuty, Datadog, Grafana, Jira, Slack, and Terraform Cloud. We're expanding the integration catalog based on early access feedback — your input directly shapes what we build next.
Is my data and codebase secure?+
Security is first-class. Agents operate with least-privilege access, all actions are logged and auditable, and you control exactly what each agent can see and do. Enterprise plans include VPC isolation and custom data residency. We'll publish our full security posture before the public launch.
When does AgentHire launch, and what does early access get me?+
We're targeting a closed beta in Q3 2025. Early access members get priority onboarding, locked-in founding pricing (significantly below our launch rates), and a direct line to shape the product roadmap — including which agents we build next. Signing up is free and there's no commitment required.
